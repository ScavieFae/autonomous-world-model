## Cycle 6

### e022a-sf-action-weight: Reduce action weight during SF steps (2.0→1.0)
**Verdict:** REJECTED
**Reasoning:** "Same family as e018d/e021b — gradient balance manipulation within truncated BPTT. 0/3 experiments in this family improved."
**What would change the verdict:** Full BPTT implementation. Or evidence that action weight specifically (not gradient balance generally) is the issue.

### e020b-sf-ratio-30: SF ratio 30%
**Verdict:** REJECTED
**Reasoning:** "10% regressed, 20% is sweet spot. Extrapolating monotonic degradation away from 20%."
**What would change the verdict:** Evidence that the failure mode of 10% (too little exposure) and 30% (too much) are different. Currently speculative — the Director assumed a symmetric inverted-U without testing the right side.
**Note:** This rejection is debatable. The Director assumed monotonic behavior from 1 data point on each side. 10% fails because the model doesn't see enough of its own errors. 30% might fail for a completely different reason (gradient budget conflict) or might succeed (more diverse error exposure). The failure modes are mechanistically different. Future agents should consider proposing this with explicit mechanism differentiation.
